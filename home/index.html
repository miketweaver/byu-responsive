  • Church-going teens go further in school

    For many American teens, the road to college goes through the chapel. Sociologists from BYU and Rice University found religiously-affiliated youth are 40 percent more likely to graduate high school than their unaffiliated peers and 70 percent more likely to enroll in college. Read more.

  • A tiny lab

    This "lab on a chip" developed through faculty-student mentoring at BYU could be a blueprint for cheap, portable devices that screen for diseases on the spot, cutting out the need to send samples to a lab. Watch how it works on this video.

BYU's Army and Air Force ROTC programs will salute President Dieter F. He, in turn, will honor BYU students who have given the ultimate sacrifice in defense of their country. Read more.

How highway bridges sing – or groan – in the rain to reveal their health

A team of BYU engineers has found that by listening to how a highway bridge sings in the rain they can determine serious flaws in the structure. Employing a method called impact-echo testing, professors Brian Mazzeo and Spencer Guthrie can diagnose the health of a bridge’s deck based on the acoustic footprint produced by a little bit of water. Read how they do it.
